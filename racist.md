
*Down the road of St George's Tea Room. The sky is dull, cloudy during the evening after sunset. Drizzle gently dab the trees on the sidewalk, casting a dispiriting grey on the surrounding nature that was once lush green. The end of the road is not in sight; the storefronts that are the street's only light source are all closed, removing the last bit of vitality from this part of Port Elizabeth. Hally stands in the middle of the road.*

HALLY   (Blankly) (Speechless)  I think I've lost them. (PAUSE) Sam... I've never seen him like this. Neither he nor Willie want me anymore. I spoke out of turn, my emotions were insurmountable... I lost my judgement, and now I've hurt them. (PAUSE) If only it had turned out differently. If only I could go back ten minutes in time, to walk away before everything escalated. Or if only Sam hadn’t made those disgusting remarks. I tried so hard to give him a decent education. History, literature, science, it’s as if my teachings never even achieved anything.

Or bloody hell, if only my crippled dad didn’t miraculously recover like that. For Christ’s sake, why did I side with him over Sam? Truth be told, it’s clear who’s done more for me. And the time we had to haul my wasted dad home… I was only eight when I lost my father. 

If any of that had happened, if I had done any of those things, maybe, my life wouldn’t be in such turmoil. If only… 

Now all the good times are gone. The times when we played chess, and deliberately let poor Willie win. The times when we made a beautiful kite together, and oh, if I had known then why Sam suddenly left me… he really only ever wanted the best for me. 

But really, there’s nothing I can do now. You can’t unring a bell. It's bloody awful that happened but as always, I’ll have to suck it up and move on. As always...

I’ve been having to do that a lot lately, aren’t I? Sucking up to everyone? No! *(Stomps foot)* It doesn't have to be like this. I mustn't hold myself hostage to someone else's opinion. Why should ***I*** cater to anyone? 

I've lived a sorry life of subservience this whole time. Dad robs me of my textbook money, and I do nothing but sulk. That's wrong, and the means to fix this were right in front of me all this time! I can't believe it's taken me 17 damned years to realise that.

This is about defending myself. Once my Dad comes back, I have no duty to care for him. But I'm also defending myself from those two. *(Mirthlessly smirks)* Being my father's slave has been more than enough. And now Sam and Willie want me to be *their* slave too? 

*(Pacing)* That's outrageous. They’re my subordinates, not the other way around, and in fact, I could just tell Mum about what he did, and he'll be gone in an instant! Or even, I'll report him to the Boer police and I'll never have to see him ever again. *(A vicious grin, looking into the distance, dreamily)* 

So this is what adulthood feels like. It's always been me under everyone's thumb, but soon I'll have the freedom I never had, and people will be subservient *to me*. Oh, nothing feels better than having agency over my own life, and that was the solution all along... *(Satisfiedly serene)*

*(Calmly cynical)* My duty to the world is done. I've given many times more than I've gotten back, and it's about time I receive something in return. And done means I'm done going around with this childish ambition to change the world, because my own problems are more than enough. Even if society's unfair, we all have to accept that reality isn't always perfect. So who am I, who is anyone, to think they can reshape South Africa? 

It's taken me this long to realise — I'm not a person of magnitude. I'm not Churchill, I'm not Lincoln, I'm not Sir Alexander Fleming... and Sam is entirely mistaken that I am one. He means well, but the Blacks aren't my responsibility, and society isn't either. Someone else will help them, just not me. 

And Sam's dream of a collision-less world is just a dream. Behind our rose-tinted glasses, that's the unfortunate truth of this world. Nobody knows the steps, there's no music, the cripples... and it's not my job to change that. (PAUSE)

None of it affects me anyway.
